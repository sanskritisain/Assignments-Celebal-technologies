# Results

## Dataset Summary

The Superstore dataset was successfully imported into the `superstore_raw` table and normalized into the following tables:

* customers
* orders
* products

SQL analysis was performed using Subqueries, Common Table Expressions (CTEs), Window Functions, and JOIN operations.

---

## Query Results

### 1. Orders Above Average Sales

Orders with sales greater than the average sales value were identified successfully using a Subquery.

Result:
High-value transactions were extracted for further analysis.

---

### 2. Highest Sales Order Per Customer

The maximum sales order for each customer was identified using a Correlated Subquery.

Result:
Each customer's highest purchase value was determined.

---

### 3. Total Sales Per Customer

Total sales generated by every customer were calculated using a Common Table Expression (CTE).

Result:
Customer-wise revenue contribution was obtained.

---

### 4. Customers with Above-Average Sales

Customers whose total sales exceeded the average customer sales were identified.

Result:
High-performing customers were separated from the rest of the customer base.

---

### 5. Customer Ranking

Customers were ranked according to total sales using the RANK() Window Function.

Result:
Customer performance hierarchy was established.

---

### 6. Order Numbering within Customers

ROW_NUMBER() with PARTITION BY was used to assign sequential order numbers for each customer.

Result:
Order history was organized customer-wise.

---

### 7. Top 3 Customers

The top three customers were identified based on total sales.

Result:
The highest revenue-generating customers were determined.

---

## Final Combined Query Result

A final analytical query combining JOIN, CTE, and Window Functions was executed.

Output Columns:

* Customer Name
* Total Sales
* Customer Rank

Result:
A comprehensive customer performance report was generated.

---

## Customer Sales Insights

### Top Customers

The top five customers generated the highest revenue and represent the most valuable customer segment.

### Bottom Customers

The bottom five customers contributed the least revenue and may require targeted engagement strategies.

### One-Time Customers

Customers who placed only one order were identified and may represent retention opportunities.

### Above-Average Customers

Customers whose sales exceeded the average customer sales were identified as high-value customers.

### Highest Order Value

The maximum order value for every customer was determined successfully.

## Key Findings

1. A small percentage of customers contribute a large share of total revenue.

2. Customer ranking provides a clear understanding of business value.

3. Several customers placed only one order, indicating potential retention opportunities.

4. High-value customers can be targeted through loyalty programs and personalized marketing.

5. SQL Subqueries, CTEs, and Window Functions effectively support business analytics and reporting.

## Conclusion

The project successfully demonstrated the use of advanced SQL concepts to analyze customer sales data. The generated insights can support customer segmentation, revenue analysis, and business decision-making.
